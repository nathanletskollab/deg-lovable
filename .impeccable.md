## Design Context

### Users
**Primary:** Women 35–44 in Toronto/GTA. They arrive already partially aligned — referred, informed, or Instagram-adjacent. They are evaluating *fit*, not discovering naturopathy for the first time. They've tried conventional approaches, are looking for deeper answers, and are deciding whether Erica is *the right person* for them. They balance career, family, and self. They want ongoing care, not a quick fix.

**Secondary:** Parent-led patients (mothers bringing children); cooling but historically engaged patients returning to care.

**Do not design for:** One-visit samplers, casual explorers, users seeking transactional care.

**Mental state on arrival:** "Is Erica the right fit for me?" — not "Does naturopathy work?"

### Brand Personality
Calm · Grounded · Quietly authoritative

The anchor phrase is *"A Body in Balance Heals Itself"* — not a tagline, the worldview everything else flows from. The experience should feel: warm but intelligent, personal but clear, confident without pressure.

**This is person-led, not category-led.** We are not selling naturopathy. We are attracting people to Erica.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Light. Warm cream backgrounds (#FAF6ED), pine green (#1F3931), coral rose accent. These should feel like natural materials — paper, sage, cedar — not a wellness brand color kit.
- **Typography:** Cormorant Garamond (display/headings, weight 300–600, classical and delicate) paired with Instrument Sans (body/UI, weight 400–600, clean and readable). The serif does the emotional lifting; the sans handles clarity.
- **Visual tone:** Organic, editorial, unhurried. Real photography, not polished stock. Generous whitespace. Soft textures at low opacity. Rounded large sections with overlapping stagger. The site feels like a beautifully designed health magazine that happens to also book appointments.
- **Motion:** Subtle fade-up reveals, scroll-triggered. Nothing that calls attention to itself. Framer Motion already wired.
- **Anti-references:** Generic clinic sites, wellness startup sites with gradient illustrations, anything that feels "designed to feel wellness-y." Avoid: blue/teal palettes, gradient pills, abstract blob shapes, stock photography of women meditating, sparkle/leaf iconography used as decoration.

### Design Principles
1. **Recognition before persuasion.** Every section should make the right user feel seen before it explains anything. "This is exactly what I've been dealing with."
2. **Progressive trust.** The homepage is a guided journey: emotional entry → pattern recognition → trust → care journey → gentle conversion. Each section earns the next.
3. **Person-led warmth.** Erica speaks in first person for philosophy, approach, and reassurance. The interface speaks in third person for CTAs, navigation, and system instructions. Never mix the two voices.
4. **Clarity without simplification.** Short paragraphs, clean hierarchy, generous spacing. But never dumbed down — this audience is intelligent and knows it.
5. **Gentle conversion.** No pressure tactics. Pricing visible. Clear paths. Confidence and clarity drive action, not urgency or scarcity.
